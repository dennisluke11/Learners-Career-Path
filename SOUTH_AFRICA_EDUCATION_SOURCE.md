- **Grades 10-12** - Not compulsory but required for NSC (Matric)

## National Senior Certificate (NSC) Requirements

### Compulsory Subjects (4 required):
1. **Two Official Languages**
   - One at **Home Language** level
   - One as **First Additional Language**
   - Common choices: English, Afrikaans, isiZulu, isiXhosa, Sesotho, Setswana, etc.

2. **Mathematics OR Mathematical Literacy**
   - Mathematics (for science/engineering paths)
   - Mathematical Literacy (for non-science paths)

3. **Life Orientation**
   - Life skills, well-being, career guidance

### Elective Subjects (3 required):
Students must choose 3 additional subjects from the following categories:

#### Sciences:
- **Physical Sciences** (combines Physics and Chemistry)
- **Life Sciences** (Biology)
- **Agricultural Sciences**

#### Business & Commerce:
- **Accounting**
- **Business Studies**
- **Economics**

#### Humanities:
- **History**
- **Geography**

#### Technology:
- **Information Technology (IT)**
- **Computer Applications Technology (CAT)**
- **Engineering Graphics and Design (EGD)**

#### Arts:
- **Visual Arts**
- **Dramatic Arts**
- **Music**

#### Other Electives:
- **Tourism**
- **Consumer Studies**
- **Hospitality Studies**
- **Design**

## Grade Levels

For our application, we focus on **FET Phase (Grades 10-12)**:
- **Grade 10** - First year of FET
- **Grade 11** - Second year of FET
- **Grade 12** - Final year, NSC examinations (Matric)

## Curriculum Policy

- **National Curriculum Statement (NCS)** Grades R-12
- **Curriculum and Assessment Policy Statements (CAPS)** - Subject-specific policy documents
- **Three-Stream Model** (introduced 2024-2025):
  - Academic stream
  - Occupational stream
  - Technical stream

## Recent Changes (2024-2025)

1. **New Vocational Subjects** (Occupational Stream):
   - Ancillary Health Care
   - Motor Mechanics
   - Welding
   - Hospitality
   - Electrical Technology
   - Civil Technology
   - Technical Sciences

2. **Coding and Robotics**:
   - Being piloted in many schools
   - Intended for broader rollout

3. **Entrepreneurship Education**:
   - Compulsory in many schools

## Subject Naming Conventions

### Official Names (as per CAPS):
- **Physical Sciences** (not "Physics" - it includes both Physics and Chemistry)
- **Life Sciences** (not "Biology")
- **Mathematical Literacy** (not "Math Literacy")
- **Computer Applications Technology** (CAT)
- **Engineering Graphics and Design** (EGD)

### Language Subjects:
- Format: `[Language Name] (Home Language)` or `[Language Name] (First Additional Language)`
- Examples:
  - English (Home Language)
  - English (First Additional Language)
  - Afrikaans (First Additional Language)
  - isiZulu (Home Language)

## Notes for Implementation

1. **Physical Sciences** is a combined subject (Physics + Chemistry), but we map it to `Physics` for internal consistency
2. **Chemistry** is technically part of Physical Sciences, but listed separately for career requirements
3. **Life Sciences** = Biology (mapped to `Biology` internally)
4. Students typically take **7 subjects total** (4 compulsory + 3 electives)
5. Some subjects may vary by school/province availability
